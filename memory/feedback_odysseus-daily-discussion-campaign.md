## 🎯 Length constraints — soft-cap recalibration (Will 2026-06-07)

> *"the 150 word cap should be a soft cap because there are bound to be scenarios where 150 words just can't fully express an idea or adress everything need adressing"* — Will 2026-06-07

### Tiered word-count discipline
- **target ≤ 150 words** ⇒ default-shape ∀ comment
- **soft-cap 151-250 words** ⇒ ✓ permitted IFF every paragraph load-bearing (paragraph-removability test fails)
- **hard ceiling 300 words** ⇒ ✗ exceed except categorical-substance (full-mechanism-explanation that doesn't compress)
- **EXIT-SILENT trigger** ⇒ IF ¬ achievable ≤ 300w while preserving structural-substance ⇒ exit silent ⇒ post ¬ ready

### Paragraph-removability test (mandatory @ word-count > 150)
∀ paragraph beyond word 150 ⇒ ask: "Can this be removed without weakening the structural claim?"
- IF yes ⇒ remove ⇒ recount
- IF no ⇒ keep ⇒ paragraph earns its words
- Apply ∀ paragraph until ≤ target OR all remaining paragraphs load-bearing

### Anti-pattern (unchanged)
- structural-claim @ first-sentence ⇒ ✓ payload-first
- ✗ parallel-construction listicle stacks ⇒ LLM-shape-identifier
- ✗ "this composes with..." mapping bullets ⇒ over-justification
- ✗ 3-section structure (problem ∧ mechanism ∧ why-compose) ⇒ pick-one-move (unless mechanism-explanation is the whole substance)
- ✗ closing "worth flagging" ∨ "worth noting" soft-hedges

### Will-frame ⇐ Why soft

- hard-150 ⇒ would force compress-past-clarity OR skip-legitimate-substance
- mechanism-explanation comments ⇒ genuinely require 200-300w to convey shape
- ≤ 150w ⇒ default, ≤ 250w ⇒ allowed-w/-justification, ≤ 300w ⇒ exceptional, > 300w ⇒ exit-silent
- the 150-target IS the discipline; the 300-ceiling IS the safety
